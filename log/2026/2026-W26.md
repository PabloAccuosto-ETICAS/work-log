Surfaced a new system-side finding, **F-039**: ~5% of fully-completed evaluations (`completionPercent=100`, 5/5 ECOs driven) deliver a competency profile with only 4 of 5 scores, the global silently averaged over the subset — confirmed not a collection artifact (direct out-of-collector query of the public `last-evaluation` endpoint at +22 h still returns 4) and not drive-side (no desync, no conversation-length association); concentrated on PC/Autorregulación/Agilidad, never GI/AC. Tightened the rate with an overnight reliability probe (200 runs → pooled 13/256 ≈ 5.1%, 95% CI [3.0, 8.5]). Extended the analyzer with a `contrast-ft` subcommand (full-traversal: global primary + per-ECO Holm family) but held the canonical analysis: won't measure fairness on a system that silently drops a scored dimension while still changing. Sent Jaime a one-point query with a reproducible `curl` (mechanism? public endpoint under-exposing vs. internal?).
